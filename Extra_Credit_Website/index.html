Muddy Waters,
Rolling Stones and How
Rock Music Popularised A Common Place Proverb

The story of how blues and country musicians from 1950s popularised “rolling stones gather no moss” and cemented it as the most common proverb used in music.

1

Artist: Hank Williams

Song: Lost Highway

Genre: Country

Year: 1949

Lyrics

I'm a rolling stone, all alone and lost.

Fact

Although ‘Lost Highways is Williams’ most famous–and arguably most influential–song, he didn’t actually write it. Leon Payne, a singer from Texas, wrote and released it but the song majorly flopped before being revived by Williams.

2

Artist: Muddy Waters

Song:Rollin’ Stone

Genre:Blues

Year:1950

Lyrics

Well, my mother told my father
Just before hmm, I was born
"I got a boy child's comin', he's gonna be
He's gonna be a "Rollin' Stone"

Fact

The song outlines the story of a guy who won't settle down, you can find him calling on the ladies when their husbands aren't home.

3

Artist: Bob Dylan

Song: Like a Rolling Stone

Genre: Classic Rock

Year: 1965

Lyrics

How does it feel, how does it feel?
To be without a home
Like a complete unknown, like a rolling stone

Fact

Dylan based the lyrics on a short story he had written about a debutante who becomes a loner when she falls out of high society. The lyrics that made it into the song are only a small part of what was in the story. He got the idea from Williams and used the proverb to indicate loneliness and despair

4

Artist: Jimi Hendrix

Song: Highway Chile

Genre: Rock, Pop

Year: 1967

Lyrics

He left home when he was seventeen
The rest of the world he had longed to see
But everybody knows the boss
A rolling stone who gathers no moss.

Fact

The phrase "a rolling stone gathers no moss" was inspired by Muddy Waters’ “Rollin' Stone" and Bob Dylan’s” Like a Rolling Stone", but Hendrix used the full proverb in this song. It is also the only instance of the entire proverb in this list.

5

Artist: Don McLean

Song: American Pie

Genre: Folk Rock

Year: 1971

Lyrics

For 10 years we've been on our own
And moss grows fat on a rolling stone
But that's not how it used to be.

Fact

While American Pie was written lamenting the death of Buddy Holiday, he was most likely referencing The Rolling Stones in that line.

6

Artist: Bruce Springsteen

Song: Blinded by the Light

Genre: Rock

Year: 1973

Lyrics

Some brimstone baritone anti-cyclone rolling stone preacher from the east
He says, "Dethrone the dictaphone, hit it in its funny bone, that's where they expect it least"

Fact

This was the first song on Springsteen's first album: Greetings From Asbury Park, N.J. Columbia Records marketed him as someone who “[..] puts more thoughts, more ideas and images into one song than most people put into an album." A lot of the references in the song are personal, to include people he knew or had met on the Boardwalks, or had grown up around, or were just direct personal references to himself

As the proverb became increasingly ubiquitous in rock music, artists from others genres–pop, indie, country–started borrowing the expression mostly using it to define a person’s characteristics.

7

Artist: Elton John

Song: Philadelphia Freedom

Genre: Pop

Year: 1975

Lyrics

I used to be a rolling stone you know
If the cause was right
I'd leave
To find the answer on the road

Fact

On the single, it said this song was dedicated to "B.J.K." (Billie Jean King) and "The Soulful Sounds Of Philadelphia." The title “Philadelphia Freedom” is in honour of Elton John’s friend and fellow LGBTQ+ icon, Billie Jean King. She was one of the first women to coach a professional mens tennis team called the Philadelphia Freedoms.

8

Artist: Bon Jovi

Song: Who Says You Can’t Go Home

Genre: Rock, Pop Rock

Year: 2005

Lyrics

There's only one place they call me one of their own
Just a hometown boy born a rolling stone
Who says you can't go home?

Fact

The song went to #1 on the Country charts, the first time a rock band has done that. This won a Country Music Television (CMT) award for Best Collaborative Video. Hailing from New Jersey, Bon Jovi wanted to write a song honouring their roots. NJ has subsequently adopted the song as a banner for the state.

9

Artist: Bruno Mars

Song: Runaway Baby

Genre: Pop/ R&B

Year: 2010

Lyrics

'Cause Lord knows I'm a rolling stone
So you better run, run, run away, run away, baby.

Fact

This is a track from Bruno Mars' debut album, Doo-Wops & Hooligans.

Now, the words have become so common place that they are used as a passing line in many songs. The phrase has grown from its roots in rock music and is being adopted in common speech without people realising the rich history behind it.

10

Artist: Halsey

Song: Ghost

Genre: Alternative/Indie

Year: 2014

Lyrics

You're a Rolling Stone boy
Never sleep alone boy
Got a million numbers
And they're filling up your phone, boy

Fact

This is a track from her debut EP, Room 93. It was later included on Halsey's debut album Badlands. "It's about looking for something in someone that you can't find," she said. Whether subconsciously, unconsciously, or intentionally, Halsey uses the same connotations of playful abandon and voyeurism as Muddy Waters’ “Rollin’ Stone”
